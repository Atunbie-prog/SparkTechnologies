About Chromebooks
What exactly is a Chromebook? +

A Chromebook is a laptop that runs ChromeOS — Google's cloud-first operating system. Instead of Windows or macOS, it uses a lightweight interface designed around Google apps like Google Classroom, Google Docs, Gmail, and the Chrome browser. Chromebooks are faster to boot, simpler to manage, and generally more affordable than traditional laptops.

Can Chromebooks run Microsoft Word or Windows software? +

Chromebooks do not run Windows software natively. However, they have access to Google Workspace apps (Docs, Sheets, Slides) which function very similarly to Microsoft Office. Many Chromebooks can also run Android apps from the Play Store. If your school specifically needs Windows applications, we'll tell you honestly and help you find the right solution.

Do Chromebooks work without internet? +

Yes. While Chromebooks are optimized for cloud use, many apps — including Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides — have offline modes. Students can work without internet and sync when a connection is available. For schools with limited connectivity, this makes Chromebooks a very practical choice.

Why are Chromebooks better than regular laptops for schools? +

Three main reasons: (1) Cost — Chromebooks deliver solid performance at a lower price than Windows laptops. (2) Management — IT admins can manage hundreds of Chromebooks from a single dashboard, pushing updates and policies remotely. (3) Durability — most education-focused Chromebooks meet military-grade durability standards, designed specifically for classroom environments.
